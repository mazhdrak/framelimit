The Reality of Sub-$1,000 Gaming in 2026

We'll be straight with you: the RTX 50-series launch raised prices across the board. In 2025, you could find capable RTX 40-series laptops for $799–$899. In 2026, most of those machines have been replaced by RTX 50-series models that start $300–$500 higher. The genuine sub-$1,000 RTX 5060 market is thin.

There is one machine we can recommend without hesitation at this price: the Lenovo LOQ 15 Gen 10 at $899. It is the entry point for RTX 5060 gaming with DLSS 4 support, and it covers 1080p gaming at Ultra settings in every current title. Everything else we'd consider "budget" starts at $1,300+.

Bottom Line Up Front If your hard limit is $1,000, the LOQ 15 at $899 is your pick — it's the only credible RTX 5060 laptop at this price. If you can stretch to $1,300–$1,500, the options improve dramatically. We cover those in our Under $1,500 guide.

1. Best Under $1,000: Lenovo LOQ 15 Gen 10

The LOQ 15 is Lenovo's no-nonsense budget gaming line, and the Gen 10 with RTX 5060 is the only laptop in 2026 we can point to that clears the $1,000 bar with a current-gen GPU. The 15.6" 1080p 144Hz IPS panel is basic but adequate for 1080p gaming. The RTX 5060 at ~100W TGP is enough to run every current title at 1080p Ultra with DLSS 4 Quality enabled pushing well above 60fps in demanding games.

Compromises are real: the display is 1080p only, RAM is 16GB (upgradeable), storage is 512GB (tight), and the build quality is plastic-forward. But the thermals are solid for the price — Lenovo's dual-fan system keeps the GPU under 85°C under sustained load — and the LOQ line has a track record of reliability.

When to Stretch Your Budget

At $1,799 — $900 more than the LOQ 15 — the Lenovo Legion 5i Gen 10 adds an RTX 5070 (roughly 40% faster than RTX 5060), an OLED display, and a noticeably more premium build. That is a very different laptop for not a dramatically different long-term cost. If you can stretch, you'll be significantly happier for longer.

If $1,349 is feasible, the TUF A16 is genuinely a better laptop than the LOQ 15 in most ways. The extra $450 buys a stronger chassis, a slightly higher-TGP GPU, and more confidence in 3–4 year longevity.
