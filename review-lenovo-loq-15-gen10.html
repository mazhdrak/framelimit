Gaming Performance — 1080p Benchmarks

The RTX 5060 at 100W handles every current game at 1080p High/Ultra. DLSS 4 support means demanding titles get a free frame-rate boost — in Cyberpunk 2077 with DLSS 4 Balanced, the LOQ 15 hits a smooth 98fps average. At native performance without upscaling, expect 55–75fps in the most demanding titles.

Thermals — Better Than Expected

Lenovo's dual-fan cooling on the LOQ 15 keeps the RTX 5060 at 82°C peak — 5–8°C cooler than competing budget machines. The CPU stays at 78°C. Fan noise maxes at 44dB — acceptable for a gaming laptop. In balanced mode for everyday use it stays near-silent. The LOQ runs notably cooler than similarly-priced ASUS and Acer machines.

Display & Battery

The 15.6-inch 1080p IPS at 144Hz is functional but not exciting — 295 nits measured, 62% sRGB. It does the job for gaming, but colours are visibly muted compared to higher-tier panels. Upgrade recommendation: if display quality matters at all, stretch to the Acer Nitro V 16 at $899 for a 16-inch 1200p panel with 88% DCI-P3. The 60Wh battery gives 3.5–4.5 hours productivity. Gaming drains it in about 90 minutes.

Build & Upgradeability

Solid plastic chassis — doesn't feel cheap but isn't premium. Both RAM slots and the M.2 slot are accessible — upgrading to 32GB RAM is straightforward and recommended if you plan to keep this machine 3+ years. Port selection is adequate: 3× USB-A, 1× USB-C, 1× HDMI 2.1, Ethernet, headphone jack. No Thunderbolt, no SD card reader.

Our Verdict
If you're buying your first serious gaming laptop and your budget is firm at $1,000, the LOQ 15 Gen 10 is the correct choice. It runs cooler, games better, and is more upgradeable than the competition at this price. The display is the weak point — if you can stretch to $1,329, the ASUS TUF A16 Entry gets you a significantly better panel and thermals. But at exactly $999, nothing beats the LOQ.
