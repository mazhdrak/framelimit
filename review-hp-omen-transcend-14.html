Thermals & Noise

The Transcend 14 prioritises thermals intelligently: the RTX 5060 runs at a conservative ~60W TGP to keep temperatures and fan noise in check. GPU peaks at 78°C — remarkably cool, and the fan noise tops out at just 42dB. This is a laptop you can comfortably use in a meeting or library at full gaming performance. The trade-off is raw frames: the constrained TGP means the RTX 5060 here is slower than in larger chassis. Expect roughly 15% less performance than the same GPU in a 16-inch machine.

Display

The 2.8K OLED display is the Transcend 14's defining feature — sharper than any competitor at this size, with 100% DCI-P3 and factory calibration under Delta-E 2. At 500 nits peak it's usable in most lighting conditions. The 120Hz ceiling is the limitation for high-refresh competitive gaming, but for single-player titles, productivity, and creative work this screen is stunning. HP includes Thunderbolt 4 for external display output, so pairing it with a 4K 144Hz monitor at your desk is a seamless workflow.
